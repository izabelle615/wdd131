Elvis Presley: The King of Rock and Roll

Elvis Presley, born in 1935, became a cultural icon and is widely considered one of the most influential figures in the history of music. His music blended different genres including rock, blues, gospel, and country, which helped shape the sound of the 1950s and beyond.

Key Moments in Elvis's Career

  • 1956: Elvis releases "Heartbreak Hotel" and becomes a global sensation.
  • 1968: After the war, Elvis's comeback special revitalizes his career.
    • 1973: Elvis performs in "Aloha from Hawaii," becoming the first concert to be broadcast globally via satellite.
